# Finding a Community Voice: How JupyterHub is Learning to Speak as an Open Source Project

## Original authors

- Arielle Bennett

## Abstract

What does it take for an open source project to sound like itself? To have a voice that not only reflects the existing maintainers efforts and perspectives, but captures goals and aims for the project, draws in new contributors, and keeps them returning?

In open source projects, where time, focus, and resources are often scarce, this type of initiative can easily be deprioritized in favor of fire-fighting, or find projects lacking in contributor skills sets to make them effective.

JupyterHub, critical open source scientific infrastructure, democratizes the creation of, and access to, interactive computing environments by providing secure, scalable, and user-friendly platforms that enable individuals, teams, and organizations to craft customized environments for exploring data, developing code, and advancing knowledge without barriers. Used by millions across the globe, it is currently building out its community voice through a series of initiatives to not only set directions for the project, but build dialogue with users, and diversify its contributors as well.

This talk reflects on what JupyterHub has learnt from building these rituals, doing the deep dive, and having the discussions to develop a consistent voice, and where it might go next. If you have ever struggled with community building, culture reflections, or contributor onboarding and are looking to learn from , this talk is for you.
